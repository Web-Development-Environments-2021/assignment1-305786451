    About me

    Chavah Shavitt was born in Berlin on 29/08/1930 under the name Chavah Rom. In 1942 she was sent to London as part of the Kinder transports.
    Chavah was adopted by a local English family and lived with them for about seven years.

    After the war, her mother came to London and the two finally reunited. Then, Chavah and her mother found a small apartment in London where they both lived for a couple of years.
    As a young adult, in 1950, Chavah Joined "Habonim" youth movement where she met Gustav (Gavriel) Shavitt, which later became her husband.

    Chavah attempted to become a nurse but was not accepted, so she started studying physiotherapy (a new profession at the time).
    Chavah and Gavriel got married in 1952, and made Aliyah (immigrated) to Israel in 1955 after they finished their studies: Chavah as a physiotherapist and Gavriel as a social worker.

    In Israel, they spent their first 5 months studying Hebrew in a Jerusalem ulpan, but after they settled down in Ramat Hasharon, where she still lives today.

    The Beilinson Hospital employed her as a physiotherapist during 1956, when she founded the first physiotherapy center there. She worked in Belinson until she retired.

    During the years Chavah and Gavriel had three children: Gilon, Dotan, and Hila. Years later, they had 8 grandchildren.
    Chavah enjoys maintaining her garden, especially while listening to classical music. In post-Corona times, she used to go out a lot: to concerts, meetings with friends, and Bridge games. In addition, Chavah has a big collection of owl porcelain dolls that she accumulated over the years.

    Ans

    Q1:
    Web 1.0:
    There is only limited interaction between sites and web users. Web 1.0 is simply an information portal where users passively receive information without being given the opportunity to post reviews, comments, and feedback.
    Web 2.0:
    Enables interaction between users and sites. Enables users to contact each other more freely. Supports collaboration and information sharing.
    Web 3.0:
    Computers can interpret information like humans and intelligently generate and distribute useful content tailored to the needs of users.
    Three key differences:

    Web1.0 Web2.0 Web3.0
      Mostly Read-Only     Wildly Read-Write     Portable and Personal  
      Company Focus     Community Focus     Individual Focus  
      Owning Content     Sharing Content     Consolidating Content  

    Q2:
    Use case: Search for a place to eat lunch.
    Web 1.0:
    We will probably get websites with very straightforward designs and content related to a business. Allot of text without any user review.
    Web 2.0:
    We will get more search results with some more personal users recommendation, blogs, and not related to business content.
    Web 3.0:
    We will get recommendations with are more suited to our taste. We can enjoy all the community aspects of web2.0 but in a more tailored way.
